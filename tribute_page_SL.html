About Stephen LaBerge:

  • Stephen LaBerge was born in 1947.
  • He received a bachelor's degree in mathematics in 1967.
  • He studied psychophysiology at Standford University, where he began his research in Lucid Dreaming.
  • He received his Ph.D. in 1980.
  • In 1987 he founded the Lucidity Institute to study and teach lucid dreaming techniques.
  • He developed several techniques, like MILD. Which stands for Mnemonic Induced Lucid Dream. This technique involves waking up after five hours of sleep and developing the intention to remember that you are dreaming, before falling asleep again. This is done by repeating the phrase: "I will remember that I'm dreaming". You also need to visualize yourself acting out a dream.
  • There are other techniques, such as WILD and DILD. Wake Induced Lucid Dreaming involves waking up after 4 to six hours, staying up from a few minutes to an hour and then going back to bed. You need to relax your body, silence your inner monologue and experience hypnagogia until you fall into a dream.
    Dream Induced Lucid Dream is any dream in which you become spontaneously lucid. This is improved by having a dream journal, doing reality checks, and figuring out your dream signs.
  • LaBerge developed a series of devices to help people enter a lucid state while dreaming: DreamLight, which was discontinued in favor of the NovaDreamer in collaboration with other researchers.
  • He has produced several books and tapes about lucid dreaming, including: Exploring the World of Lucid Dreaming (1990).
